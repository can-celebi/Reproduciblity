## Experiment
- Participants are assigned to teams of two.

- Before submitting, each team member privately sends their suggested number (guess) and a justification to their teammate.

# Classification Task
- Classify the participant's level of strategic reasoning using the guidance below.

## Level 0
- Classify a participant as level 0 if the participant satisfies one of the below criteria:
	+ Participant does not exhibit any strategic reasoning.
	+ Participant randomly chooses numbers.
	+ Participant gives non-strategic reasons for picking a number (e.g., taste).
	+ Participant may consider what other players might play without best responding to it.

## Level 1
- Classify a participant as level 1 if the participant best responds to something (calculates "two thirds") but fails to realize that others will be strategic as well.

## Level 2
- Classify a participant as level 2 if the participant not only best responds (calculating "two thirds") but also realizes that others will be strategic as well.
- At level 2, the question about the extent of strategic reasoning of others can come up. That is, the participant considers how many others are level 1 and how many are level 0, then takes into account the behavior of both level-0 and level-1 participants.

## Level 3
- Classify a participant as level 3 if the participant satisfies one of the below criteria:
	+ Participant realizes that others could be level 2 and reacts by best responding to this as well. 
	+ Participant realizes that others realize that others best respond as well.
	+ Participant implicitly asks themselves how the level-2 participants think about the distribution of level-1 and level-0 participants.

## Level 4, 5, ...
- Classify a participant as level 4, 5, or higher if the participant's reasoning process continues to higher levels where more levels of best responses and higher orders of beliefs become relevant.

## Level Infinite (equilibrium play)
- If the participant repeats the reasoning process of calculating 2/3 again and again, the participant will eventually reach zero. Therefore, a participant who considers infinite levels will propose 0. 
- If the average is 50 (level-0 belief), 2/3 of that is 33 (Level-1 belief); participants responding to that calculate 2/3 again and play 22 (Level-2 belief); responding to that yields 15 (Level-3 belief). Level-4 players would therefore respond by proposing 10. Level-5 players would again calculate 2/3 and put 7, and so on for higher levels. 
- As the level of reasoning increases, the guess gets closer to 0.
- However, even though a a high level thinker participant realizes that the equilibrium is 0, their proposal may not necessarily be 0, as they have to regard the distribution of levels among the other players. If they think, for example, that the others are Level-0 and Level-1 types, they may propose similarly to a Level-2.
